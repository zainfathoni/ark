Concert Fractions

Step-by-step visual guide using the Singapore bar model method

📋 The Problem

At a concert, 2/9 of the adults is equal to 7/10 of the children. A total of 249 people attended the concert.

How many adults were there?

1
Understand the equal part

The problem says 2/9 of adults = 7/10 of children. That means some part of the adults equals some part of the children.

Adults (9 equal parts)
2/9
Children (10 equal parts)
7/10
these two parts are EQUAL in value

So: 2 adult-parts = 7 child-parts (same number of people!)

2
Pick a number for the equal part

We need a number that both 2 and 7 divide into cleanly (no fractions!).

Multiples of 2
2 4 6 8 10 12 14 …
Multiples of 7
7 14 21 …

Both divide into 14 cleanly! So let's say:

2/9 of adults = 7/10 of children = 14 people

💡 You could pick 28, 42, or 70 instead — same answer! 14 just keeps the numbers small.

3
Work backwards to find the totals

For adults: if 2/9 of adults = 14 people…

2 parts = 14 → 1 part = 7 → 9 parts = 63

For children: if 7/10 of children = 14 people…

7 parts = 14 → 1 part = 2 → 10 parts = 20

So in our example: Adults = 63, Children = 20, Total = 83

4
Scale up to the real concert

Our example gave us 83 people total, but the real concert has 249 people. How many times bigger is the real concert?

83
our example
×3
249 ÷ 83 = 3
249
real concert

The real concert is 3× bigger! Multiply everything by 3:

Group Example × 3 Real
Adults 63 × 3 189
Children 20 × 3 60
Total 83 × 3 249 ✅
🎉 Answer
189
adults attended the concert
